Strides of Endurance

In the quiet dawn before the day,
When shadows stretch in shades of gray,
A heart beats strong, though weary still,
Endurance wakes with iron will.

Through storms that rage and winds that howl,
Through bitter nights and endless trial,
The soul stands firm, no turning back,
With faith to light the darkest track.

Each step a battle, every breath,
A whispered dance with life and death,
But in the strain, in every tear,
Endurance grows, defeating fear.

For mountains move with steady hands,
And oceans bow to shifting sands,
The world may break, but not the soul,
Endurance keeps the spirit whole.

So rise again, no fear, no doubt,
For strength is born in wearing out.
The journey long, but in the end,
Endurance wins, your closest friend.
